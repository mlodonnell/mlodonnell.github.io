Em × Bronze Ensemble

Em x Bronze Ensemble is a project for one to many simultaneously playing electromagnetically prepared cymbals and gongs. What you hear in the videos are cymbals and gongs excited at their resonant frequencies using non-contact electromagnetic actuators. I record each metal percussion instrument's spectrum and feed filtered portions of that to the electromagnet. Sometimes I use a microphone and let the instruments feedback on themselves. I use SuperCollider code.

Matthew has been vibrating cymbals, gongs and sheets of metal since the early 2000's. He first used modified loudspeakers to excite cymbals by removing the paper cone and epoxying a threaded rod directly to the voicecoil and bolting the other end to a cymbal. Over many years, Matthew developed a non-contact way to excite percussion instruments. He is still perfecting the technique.

Em x Bronze Ensemble operates just before the onset of the familiar splash of a crash cymbal when struck with a mallet or a glancing blow with a drum stick. Metal percussion instruments exhbibit very rich dynamics. A cymbal or gong has on the order of a hundred resonant modes that are not harmonically related as they are in a stringed instrument or a column of air in a wind instrument. Matthew likes to explore the region of phase space before the onset of chaos.
