Theory

Torque, field, and bronze
τ = E m ⃗ × B ⃗
Torque emerges from the cross product of an applied field, an effective dipole in bronze, and the local magnetic flux density.

The ensemble treats each bronze plate as a coupled resonator, driven by carefully shaped electromagnetic fields. By controlling the phase and amplitude of E and B, the torque τ becomes a compositional parameter rather than a hidden mechanical detail.

In practice, the effective magnetic dipole m⃗ is distributed across the geometry of the cymbal or gong. Small changes in coil placement, drive frequency, and current produce macroscopic differences in trajectory, articulation, and spectral content.

The project explores these relationships at the boundary between experimental physics and performance, where feedback, saturation, and nonlinearity are not errors to be suppressed but the material of the piece itself.
